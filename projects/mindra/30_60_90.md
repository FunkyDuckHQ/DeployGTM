# Mindra — 30/60/90 Day Plan
## GTM Leadership · Matthew Stegenga

*Built from the Deniz conversation. Specific to what they actually described, not generic sales leadership theater.*

---

## The operating principle

Mindra is building an agent orchestration product for non-technical users. The product is early. The verticals aren't fully locked. The founders need structured GTM help — not someone to run a playbook that doesn't exist yet, but someone to build the playbook while selling from it.

This plan is designed for that phase: low certainty, high learning velocity, founder-close.

---

## Days 1–30: Understand the signal before sending the message

**What I'm NOT doing:** building sequences, hiring SDRs, setting up attribution dashboards.

**What I AM doing:** figuring out where the product actually wins.

### Week 1–2: Listen and map

- Shadow every active sales conversation (if there are any)
- Interview the 3–5 customers already using the product — what triggered the buy, what problem was solved, what almost killed the deal
- Map the current pipeline: who's in it, where they're from, what stage they're at
- Identify the 2–3 accounts that converted fastest and understand why
- Document what "a great Mindra customer" looks like in concrete terms (role, company stage, use case, pain)

**Deliverable:** A 1-page ICP hypothesis with supporting evidence from actual customers. Not a deck — a working document that gets updated.

### Week 3–4: First signal map

Based on the intake conversation, supply chain and performance marketing are early traction verticals. I want to understand why before committing to either.

- Talk to 3–5 supply chain buyers and 3–5 performance marketing buyers — not to sell, to learn
- For each: what triggered the conversation? What did they try before Mindra? What made them say yes or no?
- Map the signals that indicate a buyer is ready: what are they doing, hiring, announcing, or struggling with right before they need agent orchestration?

**Deliverable:** Signal map — 5–8 specific observable signals that indicate buying intent for Mindra's product. This becomes the foundation for everything in month 2.

### Week 4: Align with founders on what we learned

- Present ICP hypothesis and signal map to Deniz and the team
- Get alignment on which vertical to go deeper on first (supply chain vs. performance marketing vs. something else that emerged)
- Define what a qualified pipeline looks like — not by stage, by evidence of real buying intent

## Days 31–60: Build the smallest system that can generate repeatable signal

**The constraint:** We don't have enough data yet to build a full GTM motion. We have enough to run a series of experiments.

### Month 2 focus: 15 targeted, high-signal outbound conversations

Not a spray-and-pray list. Fifteen accounts that match the ICP hypothesis, with specific buying signals we can identify before reaching out.

- Build the target list using signal criteria from month 1
- Write outreach calibrated to each signal (not a template — an angle)
- Track: sent, replied, conversation, qualified, demo booked
- For every conversation: what triggered the yes? What was the objection? What would have changed the outcome?

**Deliverable:** 15 outbound conversations with documented learnings. By the end of month 2, we should know if the ICP hypothesis holds or needs revision.

### Parallel: First sales infrastructure

While running outbound experiments, build the minimum infrastructure needed to not lose deals:

- HubSpot configured (basic — contacts, companies, deal stages, lifecycle stages)
- A sequence for each buyer persona (2–3 touches max at this stage)
- A demo structure that maps to the pain hypothesis, not the feature list
- A "why now" document — the specific trigger conditions that make Mindra the right choice right now

**What I'm NOT building:** attribution models, lead scoring, complex workflows. Those come after we have signal.

---

## Days 61–90: Double down on what's working, kill what isn't

By day 60, we should know:
- Which signal types generate the best conversations
- Which personas are actually buying vs. just curious
- Whether supply chain or performance marketing (or a third thing) is the primary motion

### Month 3 focus: Scale the winning pattern

If the 15 outbound conversations produced 4–5 qualified pipeline opportunities, we have a repeatable signal. Month 3 is about scaling that signal systematically:

- Expand the target list based on what the signal map says works
- Start continuous signal monitoring (BirdDog or equivalent)
- Build a weekly pipeline review with Deniz — what came in, what converted, what fell out
- Define the first SDR hire criteria (if the motion is repeatable, now we can hire into it)

If the 15 conversations produced less signal than expected, month 3 is about diagnosing why and iterating — not scaling a broken motion.

**Deliverable:** A documented GTM playbook — ICP definition, signals that trigger outreach, message angles that work by persona, demo structure, handoff criteria. Written for the next hire to inherit.

---

## What success looks like

**30 days:** ICP hypothesis documented, signal map complete, founders aligned on priority vertical.

**60 days:** 15 outbound conversations complete, 4+ qualified opportunities in pipeline, first iteration of sales infrastructure live.

**90 days:** Repeatable pattern identified and documented, pipeline velocity measured, decision made on when/whether to hire first SDR.

**What I'm not promising:** A specific number of meetings or a pipeline dollar target at 90 days. That would require knowing the ICP, signal, and product-market fit that we're still discovering. Anyone who promises specific numbers at day 90 without that data is selling you a fantasy.

What I'm promising: you'll know significantly more about where Mindra wins, how to find those buyers, and what to do when you find them. That knowledge is what the next hire inherits.

## One more thing

The founders being close to early sales is a feature, not a bug. At this stage, the best pipeline insight comes from Deniz being in the room. My job is to structure that signal, not replace it. Once we know the pattern, we scale it. Before we know the pattern, we learn it together.
