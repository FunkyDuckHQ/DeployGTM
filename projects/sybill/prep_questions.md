# Sybill — Prep Questions

*Sharp questions for the next conversation. Not generic — built from what we know about the product and this process.*

---

## The posture

You've used Sybill before. That's the advantage — show up as someone who *knows* the product, not someone who did a 20-minute Google the night before. The questions below demonstrate informed curiosity, not research theater.

Keep it conversational. One or two of these per conversation is enough. Don't run through the list.

---

## On product evolution

*Use these early — positions you as someone tracking the space seriously.*

- "When I used Sybill [previously], the core value was call summaries and CRM auto-fill. Where's the product headed that surprised you in the last 6–12 months?"

- "The hardest part of AI-powered call tools is usually trust — reps not believing the AI got the deal right. How has the team thought about that?"

- "Where does Sybill sit in the stack now — is it the system of record for deal intelligence, or is it feeding into something else?"

---

## On GTM motion and the role

*Use these to understand what you're actually walking into.*

- "What does the current outbound motion look like — and where's the friction? That usually tells me more about what the role needs than the job description."

- "If you looked at the pipeline from 90 days ago versus today, what changed and what's still the same?"

- "Who does this role work closest with — is it more founder-led collaboration or is there a RevOps or sales leadership layer already built?"

---

## On fit and expectations

*Use these when the conversation turns to role specifics or at the end.*

- "What would make the first 90 days a clear win from your perspective?"

- "What have you tried that didn't work, and what's still unresolved?"

- "Is there a version of this role that's about building the system versus running the playbook? I want to understand which one is needed right now."

---

## On timing and decision process

*Use exactly one of these if you want to advance the process.*

- "What does the timeline for this look like — is there a forcing function?"

- "What else needs to happen on your side before you're ready to make a decision?"

---

## What to signal in this conversation

1. **Product familiarity** — reference specific Sybill capabilities or your prior experience naturally, not as a qualification statement
2. **Infrastructure thinking** — show that you think in systems (signals → pipeline → CRM → measurement), not just activities
3. **Curiosity without desperation** — you're evaluating fit as much as they are

---

## What to avoid

- Asking questions whose answers are on the website
- Overselling before you understand what they actually need
- Generic enthusiasm ("I'm really excited about AI-powered sales tools")
- Committing to a role you haven't fully understood yet
