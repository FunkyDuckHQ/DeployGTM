## The problem we're solving

Peregrine has a real product and real traction. The challenge isn't credibility — it's reach. The buyers who can move without strict heritage requirements exist, but finding them before competitors do requires a system, not a contact list.

The flight heritage objection is real in primes and large government programs. It's largely irrelevant in commercial NewSpace, defense prime subcontracting, and R&D-funded programs. The question is: **which accounts have active buying signals right now, and how do you get in front of them before they've already decided?**

That's what this system does.

## The GTM OS: how it works

```
Signal Layer
  BirdDog monitors 40 target accounts for:
  → Contract awards (SAM.gov, SpaceNews, DefenseDaily)
  → Funding announcements (SBIR Phase II, VC rounds, strategic investment)
  → Hiring signals (systems engineer, payload lead, mission architect)
  → New program announcements (mission selection, RFP release)
  → Leadership changes (new VP Engineering, new mission director)

       ↓ Signal fires → account becomes active

Intelligence Layer
  For each triggered account, Claude researches:
  → What program are they working on and what's the payload requirement?
  → Do they have heritage constraints or are they heritage-agnostic?
  → What's the buying process (prime, integrator, direct)?
  → Who's the decision-maker (mission architect, chief engineer, PM)?
  → What's the pain hypothesis — cost, schedule, capability gap?

       ↓ ICP fit ≥ 4/5 and signal strength ≥ 2/3 → activate

Activation Layer
  → Contact found and enriched (LinkedIn, email)
  → Signal-led outreach generated (specific to their program + timing)
  → Message sent through the right channel (email, LinkedIn, conference)
  → CRM updated with signal source, pain hypothesis, outreach angle

       ↓ Reply or engagement

Feedback Layer
  → Replied → move to pipeline, track next steps
  → No reply → park, monitor for next signal
  → Weekly review: which signals converted? Which angles got responses?
```

## The target account map

We identified 40 accounts across four segments where Peregrine can win **without** flight heritage as the primary hurdle:

### Tier 1: Commercial Earth Observation & Intelligence (12 accounts)
These operators are building out constellations and procuring payloads on commercial terms. Heritage requirements are secondary to cost, SWaP, and schedule.

*Accounts include: [commercial EO operators, analytics companies, SAR constellation builders]*

**Why now:** Several are mid-constellation build-out and actively sourcing payloads for follow-on satellites. Procurement cycles are 6–18 months. Getting in now means being on the shortlist before RFPs drop.

**Buying signal:** Funding announcements, new satellite contracts, launch manifest slots booked without payload announced.

### Tier 2: Defense Prime Subcontractors (10 accounts)
Primes building OPIR, ISR, and proliferated LEO systems need payload suppliers who can move fast and certify to MIL-SPEC without the legacy overhead.

*Accounts include: [defense primes, OPIR program integrators, SDA subcontractors]*

**Why now:** SDA Tranche 2 and 3 procurements are active. Primes are under pressure to reduce cost and schedule. A demonstrated payload supplier with real hardware in the pipeline is a strong subcontract candidate.

**Buying signal:** SAM.gov awards, SBIR Phase II contracts, SDA procurement notices.

### Tier 3: University & Research Programs (8 accounts)
NRL, JPL, AFRL, and research universities running instrument programs. Heritage-agnostic by design — they're building firsts.

*Accounts include: [research labs, university smallsat programs, AFRL divisions]*

**Why now:** Several are past SBIR Phase I with Phase II funding imminent. This is the point where they need real components, not paper studies.

**Buying signal:** Phase II SBIR awards, journal publications, conference presentations at SPIE/Small Sat.

### Tier 4: Non-Traditional Defense & Intelligence (10 accounts)
Commercial companies building space-based ISR under OTA contracts. Fast procurement, mission-focused, no legacy bureaucracy.

*Accounts include: [NRO commercial partners, intelligence community vendors, DIU awardees]*

**Why now:** DIU and NRO Commercial Systems program offices are actively awarding OTAs for commercial payload capabilities. These buyers move fast and don't require heritage they don't have themselves.

**Buying signal:** DIU awards, OTA announcements, new hire postings for "mission systems," "GEOINT," or "ISR."

## The outreach playbook

Messages are calibrated to the buyer's role and the specific signal. No generic cold email. No feature dumping. Lead with what we know about their program and timing.

**Example: Commercial EO operator, post-funding signal**

> Subject: Congrats on the raise — payload question
>
> Saw the Series B close. Assume you're now thinking hard about Tranche 2 payload sourcing.
>
> We build optical payloads at [Peregrine's SWaP envelope and resolution specs]. Lead times are [X] weeks from PO. We've got [specific milestone] in the pipeline. No heritage requirement on your end — we take on that risk.
>
> Worth 20 minutes to see if there's a fit for your next birds?
>
> — [Name], Peregrine Space

**Example: Defense subcontractor, hiring signal**

> Subject: Quick question about your payload search
>
> Noticed you just brought on a payload systems engineer. That usually means you're integrating something new or building out a capability.
>
> Peregrine builds optical payloads qualified to [relevant spec]. Fast delivery, real flight program underway. If you're sourcing for an upcoming program and don't want to wait 18 months for a heritage provider, worth a conversation.
>
> 20 minutes?
