# Fibinaci — Response Posture & Engagement Recommendation

*Built from context.md + open-loops.md. Purpose: decide how to engage before the next conversation.*

---

## The core tension

Fibinaci has an interesting thesis and they want Matthew. The problem is the engagement structure is unclear and the economic path is equity-heavy without visible product traction or near-term monetization.

This is not a "no" — it's a "not yet without more definition."

---

## What they actually need

Based on the context, they need three things in rough order:
1. Someone to translate founder-language product vision into buyer-language value propositions
2. Help identifying and landing design partners (not generic SDR work — strategic relationship building)
3. A GTM architecture for when they're ready to build pipeline

The first two are consulting work, not pipeline execution. That's a different engagement than the DeployGTM service model.

---

## The risk if you engage without structure

- Unbounded time commitment without compensation structure
- No clear milestone where engagement "succeeds" or ends
- Equity that may never vest or materialize
- Distraction from Signal Audit clients who pay cash

---

## Recommended posture: bounded advisory with clear gates

**What to propose:**

"I'm interested in working together, but I want to structure it in a way that works for both of us. Here's what I'd suggest:

**Phase 0 (before any commitment):** I review the product materials and spend one focused session with you to understand the product well enough to translate it for buyers. Based on that, I give you an honest read on where the GTM motion starts and what it requires.

**Phase 1 (if we both like what we see):** A bounded advisory engagement — 5–8 hours total, spread over 4–6 weeks. Scope: buyer language document, design partner target list (10–15 accounts), outreach posture for those accounts. Fixed deliverable, fixed scope.

**Structure:** We figure out the economic arrangement that makes sense given where you are. That might be cash, might be equity, might be hybrid. But we nail that down before Phase 1 starts — not after."

---

## What you need before responding

The open loops block real progress:
1. **NDA** — don't review proprietary materials without it
2. **Product demo or materials** — can't translate what you haven't seen
3. **Economic conversation** — equity terms, vesting, dilution from future rounds

If they can't move on all three in the next two weeks, that's signal about where you are in their priority stack.

---

## What to say next

**If the conversation was warm and they're clearly interested:**

> "I've been thinking about this since we talked. The thesis is compelling and I think I can help translate it for buyers in a way that actually gets traction. Before I commit time, I want to see the product — can you get me a demo or the materials? And let's have a quick 20-minute conversation about what the engagement structure looks like. I'm open to creative arrangements but I need to understand the shape of it before I step in."

**If they've been slow to follow up:**

> "Circling back — I want to make sure we have the right next step. NDA + product materials would let me give you a real read on where the GTM motion starts. If timing has shifted on your end, that's fine too. What would be useful?"

---

## Red lines

- Don't start any work (even light research) before NDA is signed
- Don't agree to open-ended advisory without a defined scope and economic terms
- Don't deprioritize paying Signal Audit clients for speculative equity work
