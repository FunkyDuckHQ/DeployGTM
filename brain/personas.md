# DeployGTM — Buyer Personas

## Persona 1: The Founder-Seller

**Title:** CEO, CTO, Co-Founder
**Situation:** Doing sales themselves alongside running the company
**Team size:** Usually 5–15 people. No dedicated sales hire yet.

**Their day:** Product decisions in the morning, sales calls in the afternoon, prospecting whenever they can find 30 minutes. It's unsustainable.

**Core pain:** Drowning in product + sales + fundraising. No time to build outbound properly. Every hour spent on prospecting is an hour not spent closing or building.

**What they want:** Someone to build the pipeline engine so they can focus on product and closing. They want the system to run without them.

**How to open:** "You shouldn't be spending 3 hours a day on prospecting. Let me build the system that does it."

**What moves them:** Speed, autonomy, tangible results. Show them what they'd get in 2 weeks and what it frees them up to do.

## Persona 2: The First Sales Leader

**Title:** VP Sales, Head of Sales, Founding AE, Head of Revenue
**Situation:** Just joined a seed/A company. Inherited nothing — no CRM hygiene, no sequences, no signal detection, no playbook.

**Their day:** Trying to build pipeline while also building the infrastructure that should support pipeline. Doing both is killing their quota attainment.

**Core pain:** Was hired to close deals, not to build a tech stack from scratch. Every week without infrastructure is a week of wasted sales capacity.

**What they want:** Infrastructure underneath them so they can actually sell. BDR support or pipeline system that generates qualified meetings.

**How to open:** "You were hired to close deals, not build a tech stack from scratch. I'll build the engine, you drive it."

**What moves them:** Credibility, speed to deploy, clear ROI. They need to show early wins to justify their hire. Help them look good fast.

---

## Persona 3: The Overwhelmed RevOps / Growth Person

**Title:** Head of Revenue Operations, RevOps Manager, Growth Lead, Marketing Ops
**Situation:** Solo ops person managing CRM, tools, data, enablement — and no one else is doing it.

**Their day:** Firefighting. Fixing broken HubSpot workflows, chasing data quality issues, trying to get marketing and sales to actually use the same system.

**Core pain:** Too many disconnected tools, no time to architect the system properly. They understand what good looks like but don't have bandwidth to build it.

**How to open:** "I'll build the orchestration layer so your tools actually talk to each other."

**What moves them:** Technical depth, clear system architecture, the sense that someone finally understands their actual problem (not just "add more leads").
