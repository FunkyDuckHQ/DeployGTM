# DeployGTM — Messaging Framework

## Core rules (non-negotiable)

- Lead with the specific signal. Not "I was researching your company." Tell them what you saw and why it matters.
- Bridge to pain. Don't describe what we do — describe what they're probably experiencing right now.
- Make the ask concrete. "Worth a 20-minute call?" is weak. "I do a 2-week Signal Audit for $3,500. You walk away with a working system." is real.
- Under 100 words. Every word has to earn its place.
- No AI language. No "I hope this finds you well." No "leveraging synergies." No "exciting opportunity." Write like a human who gives a shit.
- One signal, one pain, one ask. Don't pile on.

---

## Message structure

```
[Signal acknowledgment — 1 sentence, specific]
[Pain bridge — 1–2 sentences, what they're probably experiencing]
[Offer — what it is, what they walk away with, price or time commitment]
[Ask — one question or one clear next step]
```

## By persona

### Founder-Seller

**Opening angle:** You just raised / you're hiring / you're posting about pipeline — and you're probably still doing prospecting yourself.

**Pain bridge:** Most founders at your stage spend 3+ hours a day on prospecting while trying to close deals and ship product. The problem isn't effort — it's that there's no system.

**Offer bridge:** I build the system — signals, enrichment, CRM, outreach — so you don't have to.

**Sample (funding signal):**
> Congrats on the [round]. Most founders at your stage are still doing outbound manually while trying to close deals and build the product. I build the pipeline engine — signal detection, enrichment, CRM, sequences — so you can focus on closing. I do a 2-week Signal Audit for $3,500. You walk away with a working system and a list of your best accounts with active buying signals. Worth a call?

### First Sales Leader

**Opening angle:** You just joined / you're hiring reps / you inherited nothing.

**Pain bridge:** You were hired to close deals. But right now you're also building the CRM, writing sequences, and trying to figure out which accounts to call. That's two jobs.

**Offer bridge:** I'll build the infrastructure underneath you — BirdDog for signal monitoring, HubSpot fully wired up, outreach sequences live — in two weeks.

**Sample (hiring signal):**
> Saw you're hiring your first AE at [company]. Congrats. The tricky part is most early sales teams inherit no infrastructure — no signal detection, no CRM hygiene, no playbook. I build that. 2-week engagement, $3,500, you walk away with a working pipeline system your rep can actually use. Worth a conversation?

### RevOps / Growth

**Opening angle:** You're managing too many disconnected tools with no bandwidth to architect the system properly.

**Pain bridge:** The tools aren't the problem — the orchestration layer is missing. Signal detection, enrichment, CRM, outreach — they work, but not together.

**Offer bridge:** I wire up the full stack and hand you a system that actually closes the loop.

**Sample (tech stack signal):**
> Noticed [company] recently added [Clay/Apollo/HubSpot]. The tools are right — the missing piece is usually the orchestration: what triggers enrichment, what scores accounts, what fires the sequence. I build that layer. 2-week Signal Audit, $3,500, you walk away with a working system and architecture you can actually maintain. Worth 20 minutes?

## Channel guidance

**Cold email:** Use the sub-100-word format above. One message, one ask.
**LinkedIn DM:** Even shorter. 2–3 sentences max. The goal is to get a reply, not close the deal.
**Follow-up #1 (day 3):** Add one new piece of value — a relevant signal, a relevant example, a question.
**Follow-up #2 (day 7):** One sentence. "Still relevant?" or forward the original with a one-liner.
**Follow-up #3 (day 14):** Bumping or closing the loop. "Happy to park this if timing is off."

After 3 touches with no response: pause, tag in HubSpot, let BirdDog surface the next signal before re-engaging.
