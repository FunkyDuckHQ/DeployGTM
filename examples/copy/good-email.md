# Good Example: Source-Grounded Operator Copy

## Why This Is Good

- The entity is specific and resolved.
- The message uses a narrow business hypothesis.
- It avoids pretending to know more than the sources support.
- The CTA asks for a lightweight correction or confirmation, not a forced meeting.
- The copy sounds like an operator opening a useful conversation.

## Email

Subject: NewSpace buyer timing

Tyler,

I may be off, but Peregrine's hardest GTM problem does not look like broad aerospace lead gen.

It looks more like finding NewSpace teams with active build cycles, enough urgency to buy before the next milestone, and enough technical risk that the usual "come back after flight heritage" objection needs to be handled early.

That is the kind of account logic I help turn into a repeatable system: signals, scoring, account research, and message tests that show which assumptions are actually working.

Worth comparing notes on where that logic lives today?
